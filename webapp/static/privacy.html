Privacy

Last updated: 26 June 2026 · Alpha

Gotcha records the audio of meetings you choose, turns it into a transcript, and generates a short cited report. Your meetings are sensitive, so here's exactly what we collect, where it goes, and how to delete it — in plain language.

Alpha status. Gotcha is an early alpha run by a small team. We collect as little as we can to make the product work, but this is not yet an enterprise-hardened service — please don't record meetings whose contents you aren't free to share with the processors listed below.

What we collect

  • Account info — your email address and name, from Google sign-in or an email sign-in link. No passwords are stored.
  • Meeting audio — the two audio tracks (your mic and the call audio) that you record and upload, only when you press Record.
  • Derived content — the transcript and the generated report.
  • Usage — how many minutes you've processed, to enforce fair-use caps.

We do not run a meeting bot, we do not join your calls, and we do not capture anything when you're not actively recording.

How your meeting is processed

When you choose to process a recording, your audio and transcript are sent to a small set of service providers to do the work:

  • Sarvam AI — speech-to-text transcription of the audio.
  • Anthropic (Claude) — turns the transcript into the cited report. Real meetings are processed on a no-training tier, so your content is not used to train AI models.
  • Amazon Web Services — hosts the backend and stores your data.
  • Google — sign-in (if you use "Continue with Google").
  • Resend — sends sign-in-link emails (if you use email sign-in).

Storage & security

  • All traffic is encrypted in transit over HTTPS.
  • Your data is stored on encrypted disk volumes, isolated per account. We do not add a separate application-level layer of encryption on top of that yet.
  • API keys for the services above are held only on our server and are never exposed to the app or browser.

Retention & deletion

Your recordings and reports belong to you. You can delete any meeting at any time from inside the app — that removes its audio, transcript, and report from our storage. To delete your whole account, contact us and we'll remove your data.

Recording consent — your responsibility

You are responsible for having the right to record a given meeting. Laws differ by region: some require that all participants consent to being recorded. Use Gotcha only where you're allowed to, and tell participants when that's required.
